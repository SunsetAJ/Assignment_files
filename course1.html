    BLACK AND WHITE

    Step 1:

    Open Photoshop on your Computer and Load up the image you wish by going to;
    "File > Open > (Navigate to your image)"
    This will be the image you will turn into a killer Black and White photograph.

    Step 2:

    After the image is open in Photoshop the next step is to save. Saving allows you to easily go back when you make a mistake.
    Ctrl + S or "File > Save As > (Save image where you want)"
    This will allow you to save your files.

    Step 3:

    To add the Black and White layer mask.
    Go to the bottom of the layer panel which is located the the bottom right of the screen and click on the icon that looks like a circle that is half white and half black.
    Select the layer mask that says;
    "Black & White..."
    Or go to;
    "Layer > New Adjustment Layer > Black & White..."

    Step 4:

    After you add in a Adjustment Layer a window will pop up
    Hit the two buttons that can be seen on the photo
    "Auto"
    This will change the settings of the adjustment layer to fit the image.
    You can also chance the sliders to manually adjust the settings to change the photo to your own preferences
    "Clipping Mask"
    (The box with the little arrow) This will make the adjustment layer only affect the layer below it and nothing else.
    If you like the Adjustment Layer to affect all your layers the leave out this step.

    Step 5:

    After you have edited the photo it is time to export it to a appropriate file type.
    "Ctrl + S or Ctrl + Shift + S"
    Give the image a appropriate name and in the 'Save as type' select JPEG
    A pop up window will appear changing the slider will increase or decrease the quality of the image.
    Note that the higher the number the larger the size of the file will be.
    Hit "ok", congratulations, you have created a Black and White image.

    SEPIA

    Step 1:

    Firstly open the image you would like to make into a sepia image.
    "Ctrl + O" or "File > Open > (Locate File)"
    You can use your own image or one of the images provided in the download link in the
    footer below.
    Remember to always save your work
    "File > Save"

    Step 2:

    Next add an Hue/Saturation Adjustment Layer to the image.
    "Layer > Adjustment Layers > Hue/Saturation"
    Or you can go to the little circle at the bottom of the layers panel.
    The Hue/Saturation Layer allows you to edit a images color.

    Step 3:

    Click on the little icon in the pop up menu of the Adjustment layer called;
    "Colorize"
    This will allow you to change to put a color filter over the whole image.

    Step 4:

    Change the Hue slider to a yellow tint, in this case around 42.
    This will give the image the classic Sepia look.

    Step 5:

    After you have edited the photo it is time to export it to a appropriate file type.
    "Ctrl + S or Ctrl + Shift + S"
    Give the image a appropriate name and in the 'Save as type' select JPEG
    A pop up window will appear changing the slider will increase or decrease the quality of the image.
    Note that the higher the number the larger the size of the file will be.
    Hit "ok", congratulations, you have created a Sepia image.

    VIGNETTE

    Step 1:

    Firstly open the image you would like to make into a sepia image.
    "Ctrl + O" or "File > Open > (Locate File)"
    You can use your own image or one of the images provided in the download link in the
    footer below.
    Remember to always save your work
    "File > Save"

    Step 2:

    Select the circle Marquee tool (M) or go to the tool panel and click and hold the Marquee tool and select the circle select tool
    Click and drag from one corner to the other.
    This will create a oval shape selection of the image.

    Step 3:

    While the selection is active inverse the selection
    "Ctrl + Shift + I"
    Now create a new layer;
    "Shift + Ctrl + N"
    With the new layer selected use the fill tool (G) to fill the selected area of the image.

    Step 4:

    Use "Ctrl + D" to deselect the selection.
    With the layer selected go to;
    "Filter > Blur > Gaussian Blur"
    A pop up menu will appear the more the value the greater the blur. Make the Radius value a high number,
    For this image, a value of 817 was used. This creates the vignette effect.

    Step 5:

    After you have edited the photo it is time to export it to a appropriate file type.
    "Ctrl + S or Ctrl + Shift + S"
    Give the image a appropriate name and in the 'Save as type' select JPEG
    A pop up window will appear changing the slider will increase or decrease the quality of the image.
    Note that the higher the number the larger the size of the file will be.
    Hit "ok", congratulations, you have created a vignette image.
